## A Warrior's Check-In

Is something on chat stirring a strong reaction in you?

Then pause before hitting __Send__.

Breathe.

How are you feeling?

Now answer, to yourself, the following:



### 1. Am I clear on my intention?

Ask yourself: *Why am I posting this?*

- To inform? To connect?
- To be heard?
- To vent?
- To change someone's mind?

There are no wrong answers. But know your intention. It helps you post from a place of choice rather than reaction.

### 2. Am I triggered?

Notice your body.

Is there a charge in your chest, your jaw, your gut?

Do you feel heat, urgency, or an impulse to fire back? That is important data.

It does not mean you should stay silent. It means your post deserves more care, not less.

Consider: *What is this charge telling me about myself?*

### 3. Am I speaking from my own experience?

Ask: *Can I say this as an "I" statement?*

If your message is mostly about what others are doing, saying, or thinking, it may be worth turning the lens inward first.

### 4. Am I owning my judgements?

Are you making judgements of another man or group?

Can you name them as yours?

Judgements are not forbidden here, but are you projecting them as objective truth, or as accusations?

That's different from owning them as your own response.

### 5. Would I say this in a circle?

There is a real human being about to read your message. Probably not to dissimilar to you.

Imagine you are in a physical circle with him and the other men in the chat.

Would you say these words in the same way, to their faces, with them looking back at you?

If not, consider rewriting.

### 6. Am I adding to the heat or to the light?

Hot topics will arise.

Ask yourself honestly: *Is my post likely to help men feel more connected and understood? Or more divided and defensive?*

Both outcomes are a choice.

### 7. Have I considered what I might be missing?

The Clearing process invites us to distinguish *data* from *judgement* from *feeling*.

Have you separated these in your own mind before sharing them with the group?
